Pornographic Nature
2014
Video, 10:40
The video is made from found footage taken from the dead times in porn movies: shots of scenery and atmosphere. Not a single person is shown. A voice over reads in a British accent a text about the beauty and importance of forgetting, taking quotes from different sources trying to make a case for forgetting.
All the footage is taken from websites that are held by ThinkGeek corporation.
The length is the length of an average visit to Pornhub.

How To Become One with a Monument
2014
Performance
The city of Chicago is renowned for its monumental architecture.
I, as a foreigner in the city, feel both in awe and excluded by the monuments
In a series of performances I try to attach myself to the monuments using osmosis.
The band is there in order to keep the head locked in place, always touching the monument.
Each performance ended when I was asked what I was doing or asked to leave.

A Map Called Home
2014
Vinyl, antique rake heads, text
An ongoing performance in which I wear the harness with the rake heads attached,
attempting to systematically walk the entire grid of Chicago starting from the 0/0 point (State and Madison)
The rakes slowly grind away.
The only documentation is the objects collected by the rakes,
bits of texts about the conversations along the road, and the echo bouncing from the buildings.

Let Me Ask the Questions
2013
Digital printout, tomatoes, index cards, envelopes, paper bags
In front a large low quality print of a theatre curtain with a single microphone, I take out an envelope from a back pocket. Reading each sentence from a new index card, I recite a text about professional personality tests and instructions to the performance. Each member of the audience gets a small bag with 19 grape tomatoes. After they get the bags, I explain that I will read the questions from the test that are not calculated in the score of the original. Every time a viewer says yes to a questions they throw a tomato at me. At the end of the questions, I pass through the audience and ask each one how many tomatoes they have left in the bag. According to that number they get an envelope with a "reading" of their personality. These readings are quotes from sources differing from greek and modern philosophy to pop music. Taken out of context the quotes seemly oddly fitting to each receiver.

Treated Space
2014
Vinyl, foam, wine glasses
The entire room is covered in neutral grey vinyl. Cupping therapy is applied to the second skin of the walls. The treatment ends when the walls decide, throwing the cups to the ground. The floor next to the walls is covered with vinyl and foam tiles made especially to catch the falling cups.

Spacial Problems
2012
Radio transmission, digital prints
In 2003 a radio transmission was sent to 6 planets that are presumed that they might hold life. Into this message I have inserted a Rosetta Stone about language. The writing is in a font that I made specifically for this project. The original message ends with a series of questions about the the receivers, I have replaced those questions with personal messages by people surveyed for this project. The project revolves around unseen transmission, I try to render the invisible into the visual. The final step in the first stage of the project will be building a transmitter to send the answered to space.

Wine/Light/Incarnate
2011
Fifteen wine glasses are set in a row on the floor.
They are arranged in a gradient from pure red wine to pure white wine. An energy saving light bulb sits in each glass.
The electricity cables hang down from the ceiling.
The room slowly fills up with the smell of the wine evaporating.
The usually harsh light of the light bulbs is filtered and warmed by the shade of the wine.
